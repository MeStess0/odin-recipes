Egg

Home

A single fried egg with a bright, sunny yellow yolk and crispy white edges cooking in a frying pan.

Ingredients

  • Egg
  • Butter
  • Seasoning
  1. Use the right pan: pick a good nonstick or well-seasoned cast-iron skillet and clean, dry surface.
  2. Heat the pan gently: set the burner to low–medium (aim for no loud sizzling). Add about 1 tablespoon (≈14 g) of butter for one egg and let it melt until it’s foaming but not browned.
  3. Crack the egg into a small bowl, then slide it into the butter (this keeps the yolk intact and avoids shell bits).
  4. Cook low and slow: keep the heat low so the whites set evenly. If you want a runny yolk and soft whites, cover the pan for ~1½–3 minutes until the white is set. For extra-even cooking, spoon melted butter over the top (basting).
  5. Finish & season: remove from heat when whites are set to your liking, season with salt and pepper, and serve immediately.
